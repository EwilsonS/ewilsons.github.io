    Whew, that’s a mouthful. So, who am I? My story begins in Kalamazoo, MI where a General Motors man and a lovely nurse raised three boys (me, being the youngest) to work hard, value education and always do the right thing. We were a normal family who did normal things, but i wasn’t satisfied. I remember asking my mother throughout the years; “What am I good at?” She would always give me the boilerplate mom answers. “You’re smart, kind… good at math.” I carried this uncertainty with me through high school (where I graduated at the top of my class), though college (where I changed majors a few times, dropped out, went back and still graduated in four years), and through a promising management career that just didn’t make me happy, but did help me figure it out. I’m good at learning.

    I would describe myself as a thinker. Learning new things is exciting to me to me, personal growth is essential. While my education and occupational history are diverse, the need to set and achieve goals to become better remained constant.

    After about eight years in my previous career, I hit a plateau. I stopped growing and that’s a problem. Web development was always a thing I did in the background. Pursuing it professionally seemed an impossible feat. Little did I know, coding is just like learning a language, right? Slowly but surely I inched my way to web dev’s door and let myself in. Once I got my footing, I enrolled in the University of North Carolina’s Coding bootcamp to tie it all together for me. Here I am.

    I specialize in working with the MERN (mongoDB, express, react and node) stack. Also, I’m a champion of modern methodologies such as Agile and MVC. Please afford me the opportunity to discuss with you in person my possible contribution to your team. I am confident that you will be pleased with my work ethic, knowledge and enthusiasm.
